About Bidya Positioning IQ

Every business has a story.
Most are too busy to find it.

Most brand positioning work starts in the wrong place. It starts with frameworks — archetypes, matrices, competitor maps. All useful. None of them the thing itself.

The thing itself is always more specific. It's the reason you started. The customer you keep thinking about. The thing you'd do even if nobody was watching. Bidya Positioning IQ is built to find that — and turn it into a strategy you can actually use.

Why most brand positioning doesn't stick

There's a lot of brand strategy that looks right. The words are good. The slides are clean. But six months later, nobody can remember what the brand actually stands for — because it was built around what sounded smart, not what was true.

The brands that last — the ones that build genuine loyalty — tend to have one thing in common: they knew who they were before they tried to tell anyone. Bidya Positioning IQ is designed to help you figure that out first.

What makes this different

We don't start with your competitors. We don't start with market gaps. We start with you — your origin, your conviction, the transformation you actually deliver. Everything else follows from that.

The output isn't a slide deck. It's a playbook, a case study written about your brand, and content that sounds like you — because it is. No jargon without explanation. No advice that could apply to any brand.

The brands that shaped this thinking

01
Tech · EdTech · $17B valuation

Duolingo

Duolingo didn't build a better language app — it built a character. By treating Duo as a real presence in internet culture, they expanded from "language learners" to "people who want to feel something." Their marketing stunts aren't random — every one is a calculated extension of a consistent brand identity that's playful, persistent, and a little unhinged in exactly the right way.

02
Outdoor · Lifestyle · Values-led

Patagonia

Patagonia's positioning is so clear that their marketing team's hardest job is restraint. When your brand is built on a genuine belief — environmental responsibility, long-lasting product — every campaign writes itself. They've turned their values into a business model and their customers into advocates. The lesson: conviction isn't a risk. Inauthenticity is.

03
SaaS · Productivity · Community-led

Notion

Notion's growth is a masterclass in positioning around belief rather than features. They didn't build a notes app — they built a philosophy about how thinking and work should connect. Their early users weren't just customers; they were evangelists for a different way of working. That's what happens when positioning is honest about what you're really offering.

04
Eyewear · Fashion · Solitude-led

Lohause

Lohause doesn't post content — it breathes it out. Every caption reads like a journal entry by someone who sees the world slightly differently. The product almost becomes incidental; what you're buying is a perspective. This earns a different kind of loyalty — the customer doesn't just like the brand. They feel seen by it.

05
Hospitality · Food · Memory-led

Kaprica

No ads. No influencers. No moments engineered for sharing. Kaprica grew by becoming a place people needed in their lives — for first dates, hard weeks, birthdays. It kept being itself, consistently, until the right people showed up and stayed. That's not luck. That's positioning made at the level of the experience itself.

06
Coffee · Indie · Mythology-led

BKCoffeeshop

BKCoffeeshop built a mythology around coffee — complete with interplanetary menu items and a running cast of characters. Nobody else could copy it because nobody else built that specific universe. That's what great positioning does: it makes imitation pointless.

Why Is Duolingo So Crazy?

How one app built a massive brand not by competing on features — but by becoming a character in internet culture.

Storytelling model: Serial / World-building

The short version

Duolingo stopped being an education app and started being a game. Then it stopped being a game and started being a media company. Their mascot Duo showed up at concerts, faked its own death, and went viral on LinkedIn.

The question isn't how they got away with it. It's why it worked — and what it tells us about how positioning compounds.
